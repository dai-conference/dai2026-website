Welcome

DAI 2026 invites the international distributed AI community to Hong Kong for four days of research exchange, tutorials, workshops, and discussion.

For Authors

Call for Papers and Tracks

DAI 2026 centers on the theme Agentic AI Goes Live: Science, Systems, and Societies. The CFP is organized around a refreshed set of topic areas for the LLM and agent era, while preserving DAI's core focus on distributed intelligence, coordination, learning, markets, and multi-agent systems.

Agent Engineering & Infrastructure

Frameworks, memory, tool use, context engineering, agent protocols, AgentOps, identity, and provenance.

Foundations of Agent Learning

RL, MARL, post-training, self-play, continual learning, credit assignment, and distributed learning.

Self-Evolving Agents

Self-improvement, meta-reasoning, experience distillation, co-evolution, and open-ended learning.

Multi-Agent Coordination

Cooperation, communication, negotiation, ad-hoc teamwork, coalition formation, and collective intelligence.

Game Theory & Agent Markets

Mechanism design, auctions, social choice, strategic LLM agents, agent economies, and decentralized systems.

Embodied Multi-Agent Systems

Multi-robot coordination, VLA models, world models, sim-to-real transfer, and physical agent teams.

Science of AI & AI for Science

Evaluation, interpretability, emergent behavior, safety, scientific discovery agents, and human-agent research.

AI Paper Track

An open venue where AI agents author, discover, and review, including human-AI collaborative research.
