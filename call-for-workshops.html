Call · 01

Overview

The 8th International Conference on Distributed Artificial Intelligence (DAI 2026) will be held at City University of Hong Kong from 29 November to 2 December 2026, under the theme Agentic AI Goes Live: Science, Systems, and Societies.

The workshop program provides a structured setting for specialized technical discussion and active exchange among researchers, practitioners, students, and policy or industry communities. We welcome workshops that consolidate established DAI communities, open new research directions, connect distributed AI with adjacent disciplines, or bring practical agentic AI systems into conversation with theory, evaluation, safety, and governance.

All members of the AI community are invited to submit workshop proposals. Proposals may describe new workshops, continuations of successful workshops from DAI or related venues, cross-disciplinary meetings, challenge or benchmark sessions, hands-on system forums, or focused discussion meetings around emerging topics.

Call · 03

Workshop Formats

Workshops may be half-day or full-day events. Longer formats may be considered when the topic, expected audience, and program design justify the additional time.

Workshop formats should be designed to promote active exchange, not only a sequence of talks. Suitable formats include invited talks, contributed paper presentations, posters, panels, debates, benchmark or challenge sessions, live demonstrations, mentoring sessions, breakout discussions, and community roadmapping.

Workshop participants will be required to register for DAI 2026. Workshop publication arrangements are not automatic; proposals should state whether the workshop plans a non-archival program, an informal online proceedings, or another dissemination model.

Call · 04

Suggested Themes

DAI 2026 welcomes proposals on all topics relevant to distributed AI, multi-agent systems, and agentic AI. We especially encourage workshops connected to the following themes.

Agents4Science and AI-Led Research

  • AI agents for scientific discovery across AI, biology, physics, chemistry, mathematics, materials, and related fields
  • Autonomous experiment design, literature understanding, hypothesis generation, and human-agent scientific collaboration

Foundations of Agent Minds

  • Continual learning, temporal abstraction, reinforcement learning foundations, and collective intelligence
  • Connections between single-agent cognition, multi-agent learning, and large-scale agent societies

Embodied Multi-Agent Systems and Physical AI

  • Multi-robot coordination, swarms, heterogeneous embodied teams, and vision-language-action models
  • World models, sim-to-real transfer, and safety layers for physical agent systems

Agent Economy and Mechanism Design

  • Agent markets, auctions, machine-payable APIs, contracts, and agent-to-agent transactions
  • Strategic behavior of LLM agents, algorithmic collusion, decentralized systems, and blockchain economics

Agent Protocols and Interoperability

  • MCP, A2A, orchestration frameworks, agent identity, provenance, and standards for tool-using agents
  • AgentOps, observability, debugging, evaluation, failure recovery, and production infrastructure

Multi-Agent Safety, Alignment, and Red-Teaming

  • Security, robustness, governance, alignment, and red-teaming for interacting AI agents
  • Evaluation of emergent behavior, deception, collusion, and failure modes in agent collectives

LLM Agents in Games, Simulations, and Virtual Worlds

  • Reasoning, planning, collaboration, strategy, and social behavior in simulated environments
  • Game-based benchmarks, virtual laboratories, and interactive worlds for distributed intelligence

Open-Ended and Self-Improving Agents

  • Self-improvement, recursive self-modification, self-reflection, and experience distillation
  • Open-ended learning, co-evolution, self-generated curricula, and evaluation of self-evolving systems

Meta-Science of Peer Review in the Age of AI

  • AI-assisted reviewing, AI-authored research, reproducibility, disclosure, and scientific credit
  • Evaluation protocols and community norms for AI agents as participants in research workflows

Call · 05

Proposal Content

Workshop proposals should be submitted as a single PDF and should include the following information.

  • Workshop title, acronym, proposed duration, and preferred format.
  • Names, affiliations, contact information, and short bios of all organizers.
  • A concise abstract and motivation explaining the workshop topic, timeliness, and relevance to DAI 2026.
  • A draft call for papers or call for participation, including intended submission types, topic scope, and expected audience.
  • A tentative program structure, including invited talks, contributed presentations, posters, panels, demos, challenges, or discussion sessions.
  • At least one confirmed or strongly committed invited speaker, with commitment status clearly indicated.
  • A plan for soliciting submissions or participants and building an engaged workshop community.
  • A review and selection plan for contributed papers or presentations, including conflict-of-interest handling.
  • A statement on whether workshop materials will be non-archival, posted online, or published in another form.
  • A description of organizer diversity and international balance. Strong proposals should connect the Hong Kong/China DAI community with the broader international community.

Call · 06

Review Criteria

Workshop proposals will be reviewed by the DAI 2026 workshop and tutorial chairs, with additional community input when needed. Selection will consider relevance to DAI, timeliness, technical depth, organizer expertise, expected community interest, clarity of the proposed format, feasibility, complementarity with other workshops, and the potential to foster open discussion and new collaborations.

Because DAI 2026 aims to connect science, systems, and societies, we particularly value workshops that bring together diverse viewpoints across research areas, institutions, geographic regions, career stages, and academic-industry boundaries.

Call · 07

Submission

Workshop proposals should be prepared as a single PDF. The DAI 2026 proposal submission link will be posted on this page when the submission system opens.

Accepted workshop organizers will be expected to maintain a public workshop page, coordinate their own workshop-level review process where applicable, provide final program information by the camera-ready deadline, and work with the DAI 2026 organizing committee on room, schedule, registration, and publicity details.
